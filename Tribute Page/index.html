Alan Turing

Father of modern computing.

Alan Turing (1912-1954) is widely regarded as the father of computer science and artificial intelligence.

Here's a time line of Allen Turing:

Allen made significant contributions to the development of modern computing devices. Turing proposed the theoretical foundations of computation with his 1936 paper, "On Computable Numbers," which introduced the concept of the universal Turing machine. This machine model laid the basis for the design of modern computers. During World War II, Turing worked at the Government Code and Cypher School at Bletchley Park, where he played a crucial role in breaking German and Italian codes, significantly impacting the war effort. His work on the theoretical limits of computation, particularly the halting problem, and his development of the Turing test for measuring machine intelligence, have had a lasting impact on the field of computer science.
